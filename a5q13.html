Malware: Malicious software such as viruses, worms, ransomware, or spyware designed to damage or disrupt systems.

Phishing: Deceptive emails or messages aimed at tricking users into providing sensitive information such as passwords, credit card numbers, or personal data.

Denial of Service (DoS) / Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS): Overloading a system or network with traffic to make it unavailable to users.

Sabotage: Disrupting the normal operations of a business or critical infrastructure, such as power grids, healthcare systems, or transportation.

Man-in-the-Middle (MITM) Attacks: Intercepting and potentially altering communications between two parties, often to steal information or inject malicious content.

SQL Injection: Inserting malicious SQL queries into a website's input fields to gain unauthorized access to a database.

Ransomware: A type of malware that encrypts the victim's files, demanding payment (ransom) for the decryption key.

Cross-Site Scripting (XSS): Inserting malicious scripts into web pages that can execute in the user's browser, often to steal cookies or session data.

Password Cracking: Using brute force or other methods to guess or crack weak passwords to gain access to systems.

Financial Gain: Cybercriminals may steal financial data, funds, or demand ransom from individuals or businesses.

Espionage: Stealing sensitive or classified information, often for political or corporate advantage.
